LiveEdge for YouTube (Chrome extension) — last updated: July 8, 2026

1. What the extension does

LiveEdge monitors YouTube live-stream pages and, when the player falls behind the live edge (buffer drift), automatically jumps playback back to the live edge and gently speeds up playback to trim the local buffer. It can also show a small on-page status panel with your current latency and open a popup with live session statistics.

Honest limitation: the extension does not remove the broadcaster's original transmission delay. That delay is created by the encoder and CDN of whoever is broadcasting, before the video ever reaches your browser. The extension only corrects the extra drift that accumulates in your local player.

2. Privacy — what we collect

Nothing. In detail:

  • No personal data is collected, stored, or transmitted anywhere.
  • No analytics, telemetry, tracking pixels, or third-party services of any kind.
  • No browsing history is read. The extension only runs on youtube.com pages.
  • Your settings (enabled state, check interval, buffer optimizer, panel visibility) are stored with chrome.storage.sync, which lives in your own browser profile and, if you are signed into Chrome, syncs through your Google account. The developer never sees them.
  • A cumulative counter of time saved (a single number), a one-time "first live seen" flag, and a small table of learned network-calibration values (the safe latency cushion per video quality, e.g. "1080p: 2.5s" — technical numbers that expire automatically after 7 days) are stored with chrome.storage.local — on your device only, never transmitted, and deleted when the extension is uninstalled.
  • Other statistics shown in the status panel exist only in the page's memory and are discarded when the tab closes.

3. Permissions explained

  • storage — saves your settings so they persist between sessions.
  • Access to youtube.com — required to detect the "LIVE" badge state and interact with the video player on watch pages. The extension does not read, alter, or collect any other content from the page.
